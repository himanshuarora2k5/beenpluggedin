Mastering to -14 LUFS: The Streaming Standard

By Core Engineering • Post-Production

If you've ever uploaded a podcast or a track to Spotify and noticed it sounds significantly quieter (or more distorted) than professional tracks, you've likely fallen victim to streaming normalization algorithms.

What are LUFS?

LUFS stands for Loudness Units relative to Full Scale. Unlike peak meters (which only measure the absolute loudest instantaneous point of a waveform), LUFS measures the perceived loudness of a track over time. It models how human ears actually hear volume.

The -14 Target

Most streaming platforms (Spotify, YouTube, Apple Podcasts) have settled on -14 LUFS as their normalization target. If your track is louder than -14 LUFS (say, -8 LUFS), the platform will turn your track down to match -14. This means all the dynamic range you destroyed by compressing your track heavily was wasted, and your track just sounds flat and quiet.

How to Hit the Target

Use a loudness meter plugin on your master bus. Adjust your mix so that the "Integrated LUFS" (the average loudness over the entire track) sits right around -14 LUFS, with your true peaks never exceeding -1.0 dBTP (Decibels True Peak) to prevent clipping during lossy conversion.
