The Audiophile's Lexicon

Understanding the core terminology of sound engineering is the first step toward mastery.

Sample Rate & Bit Depth

Sample rate (measured in kHz) determines the frequency response of your digital audio, while bit depth (usually 16 or 24-bit) controls the dynamic range. For professional podcasting, a standard of 48kHz and 24-bit depth provides plenty of headroom without artifacts.

Plosives & Sibilance

Plosives are harsh bursts of air caused by hard consonant sounds hitting the capsule. Sibilance is the high-frequency harshness from 'S' or 'Sh' sounds. Using a quality pop filter and engaging a de-esser are crucial steps in mitigating these issues.

Phantom Power (48V)

Condenser microphones require active electronics to operate their internal impedance converters. Phantom power, typically 48 volts supplied via XLR cable from your audio interface, powers these components.

Proximity Effect

A phenomenon inherent in directional microphones where low-frequency (bass) response increases as the sound source moves closer. Broadcasters use this to achieve a deep "radio voice," requiring careful distance management.
